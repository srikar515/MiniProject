Basara Saraswati Temple

A temple dedicated to learning and worship of the Goddess of learning, it is a marvelous example of architecture of the Chalukya dynasty. Dedicated to Goddess Gnana Saraswati, Basar Saraswati Temple is situated at confluence of River Godavari and River Mangira in Basar. Believed to be the oldest temple of goddess of knowledge in India, it is said that Ved Vyas performed penance at the temple built at the very site. As per the legend, he used to make idols of Goddess Lakshmi, Kali and Saraswati with sand here. The recent structure of the temple is said to be designed by the Chalukya kings. The temple is visited in large number during the festivals of Vasant Panchami and Navratri.

Kuntala Waterfall

A beautiful waterfall located amid equally gorgeous views of Sahyadri Mountain Range nearby Neredikonda Village, Kuntala Waterfalls are considered to be the highest waterfalls in Andhra Pradesh. As per the legend, the waterfalls are named after Shakuntala, who was believed to have fallen in love with king Dushyanta, at the very site. Also it is said that Shakuntala used to take bath in this falls. Located in the proximity to the spot is a Someshwara Swami Temple which attracts devotees in large number especially during Mahashivratri
